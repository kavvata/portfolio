## Passionate about art.

I believe that wherever there is a human touch, expression inevitably follows. I see this in the oldest works of art. The greatest museums in the world are filled with the products of carpenters, painters, glassmakers — labor.

## I feel the same exists within systems.

Behind every routine, data structure, wireframe, and layout lies a decision, shaped by human intention. With each decision, moods, personalities, and the characteristics of an environment are expressed — sometimes revealed in commits and comment blocks — often evident in the source code itself.

Yet a developer’s work is not made of source code alone. There exists an entire universe of context that cannot be translated into code, documentation, or prompt files. Naur argued that these artifacts are static, a contradiction to the fact that the world is a verb. Problems mutate constantly.

## So I seek to build systems that endure through time.

Designing robust back-end architectures, maintaining automations, observability, and containerized environments for greater scalability; on the other end, applying concepts of atomic design and technical empathy to the front-end, aiming to deliver a consistent experience that speaks to the end user — while always maintaining active communication with colleagues, managers, and users, because I believe software is ultimately built through interdisciplinary conversation.

In the end, I remain curious about the forms thought can take within a system, working on my own projects and sharing what I learn along the way.
